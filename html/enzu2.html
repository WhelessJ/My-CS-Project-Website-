The Second Noble Truth: Suffering Comes From Attachment.

The Second Noble Truth, Samudaya (集諦 / Jūtai), identifies the origin of suffering as tanhā—craving or thirst—arising from ignorance (avijjā) and attachment to impermanent phenomena. This craving manifests in the desire for sensual pleasure, existence, and non-existence, binding individuals to the cyclical nature of samsāra. In academic terms, Samudaya provides a causal analysis of dukkha, situating suffering within a framework of dependent origination (pratītyasamutpāda), wherein psychological and existential dissatisfaction are not random but arise from discernible patterns of grasping and clinging. Understanding this cause is essential for progressing toward the cessation of suffering, positioning craving as both the symptom and the site of intervention.
