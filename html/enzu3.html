The Third Truth Provides A Means of Escape.

The Third Noble Truth, Nirodha (滅諦 / Mettai), asserts that the cessation of suffering is not only possible but attainable through the relinquishment of craving and attachment. This cessation, or nirodha, culminates in nirvāṇa—a state characterized by the extinguishing of the fires of greed, hatred, and delusion. Academically, Nirodha represents a soteriological claim: that the conditioned nature of suffering implies its conditional cessation. Unlike temporary relief, this truth articulates a permanent, unconditioned liberation from the cycle of rebirth (saṃsāra), reframing liberation not as an eschatological reward but as a phenomenological transformation achievable through disciplined insight and ethical practice.
