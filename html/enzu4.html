The Fourth Truth is Recognition of the Path.

The Fourth Noble Truth, Magga (道諦 / Dōtai), delineates the Noble Eightfold Path as the practical method for achieving the cessation of suffering articulated in the Third Truth. This path is structured into three core disciplines—ethical conduct (śīla), mental cultivation (samādhi), and wisdom (prajñā)—each encompassing specific practices such as right view, right intention, and right concentration. From an academic perspective, Magga functions as a prescriptive epistemology and praxis, offering a systematic approach to self-transformation rooted in empirical introspection and moral refinement. It bridges the doctrinal with the pragmatic, emphasizing that liberation is not merely a metaphysical ideal but the outcome of sustained, methodical effort.
